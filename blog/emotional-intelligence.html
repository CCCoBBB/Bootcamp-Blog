Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence is a type of intelligence referring to perceiving, understanding, controlling and managing our emotions.
We can use our emotional intelligence to manage and understand our stress, to empathize with others, to communicate more efficiently
and to respond to other individuals emotions appropriately. Emotional intelligence is also known as emotional quotient (EQ).

How is EQ different to IQ?

IQ or intelligence quotient refers to a standardized scoring system to measure an individual's intelligence. The IQ test measures an
individual's capacity for problem-solving, rational thought, and understanding or expressing complicated concepts. Compared to EQ which
is an ability to perceive, understand, control and manage our emotions.

Why is EQ important?

It is crucial to be socially and emotionally self-conscious in order to control your own emotional thoughts and behaviours and to
comprehend the feelings, wants, and concerns of others. Since emotions have an impact on both your behaviour and that of people
around you, it is necessary to be aware of them. By developing your ability to communicate, create stronger relationships, make
wiser decisions, accomplish your goals, and resolve conflicts in a healthy way, emotional intelligence can help you thrive in life.
If you need to improve your emotional intelligence, don't worry; all it takes is time and effort. Emotional intelligence can be
learnt and reinforced.
